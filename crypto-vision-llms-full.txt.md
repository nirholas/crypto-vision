# Leaderboards

Track calls, rank users, and reshape your group dynamics with Crypto Vision Leaderboards

## 📊**Leaderboard Types**

* **Calls Leaderboard** – Displays the highest-performing calls made within a selected timeframe.
* **Performance Leaderboard** – Ranks users based on their total performance points earned in the group over a specified period.

## **❓How It Works**

To check different leaderboards, use the **/leaderboard** command. You'll then be prompted to choose between viewing the **Calls Leaderboard** or the **User Performance Leaderboard**. Once selected, pick the timeframe for which you want to see rankings.

## **`📢Pay Attention To:`**

## **🥇Top Callers**

This system ranks users based on the total points they’ve earned from their calls over a given period. The more successful calls a user makes, the higher they climb in the rankings. Since points are awarded based on performance, this leaderboard showcases the most consistent and skilled callers.

## 🎯**Win Rate**

Win rate reflects how often a caller picks tokens that at least **double** in value. It’s calculated as the percentage of calls that hit a **2x return or more**.

For example, if someone makes **15 calls** and **6 of them** reach at least 2x, their win rate is **40%**. This helps distinguish those who repeatedly spot strong opportunities from those who may have benefited from a single lucky call.

## 📈**Average Gains**

This shows the typical return across all calls by averaging the results. It’s calculated by summing up all percentage gains and dividing by the total number of calls.

However, one major win can heavily influence this number. For instance, if a caller mostly lands between **1.5x and 3x**, but also scores an **80x**, the overall average may appear much higher than their usual results. It’s useful to consider this alongside other factors such as the total calls for a clearer picture.

## ⭐Performance Points System

User performance is measured through a point system that gives points for good calls and deducts points for bad ones. Every user starts with 0 points.  Here’s how it works:

* 1 point → 1x to 1.5x (weak call)
* &#x20;0 points → 1.5x to 2x (solid base call)&#x20;
* +2 points → 2x to 5x (strong call)&#x20;
* +3 points → 5x to 15x (great call)&#x20;
* +4 points → 15x to 30x (elite call)&#x20;
* +5 points → 30x or higher (legendary)

# Hardcore Mode

**To enable Hardcore Mode**, simply type `/settings`, toggle **Hardcore Mode** on, and set your desired parameters.

**Hardcore Mode** is designed for groups that prioritize performance and accountability. When enabled, it begins automatically tracking the win rate of all group members from that point forward.&#x20;

At the end of the round, members who do not meet the minimum required win rate are <mark style="color:red;">**automatically removed from the group.**</mark>

Think of it as turning your group into a performance-based game—only the consistent players stay in.

Key features include:

* **Customizable Thresholds**: Set your desired minimum win rate (default: 55%) and minimum number of calls (default: 5) required before a member becomes eligible for removal.
* **Flexible Timing**: Define your own performance evaluation period.
* **Automation**: Once configured in `/settings`, the system manages tracking and enforcement autonomously.

Hardcore Mode helps maintain high standards by ensuring only consistently performing members remain active in the group.

To check how the current round is going—and see which members are at risk of getting dropped—type `/hardcore`.
